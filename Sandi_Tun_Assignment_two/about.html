Connecting Rural Talent to the World

Tha Din was started by a group of students who realized that youth in rural Myanmar had incredible skills and a hunger for knowledge, but lacked a platform to share them.

The Library

Our library isn't just about reading; it's about digital equity. We provide offline-syncing books so students can study even when the internet is slow.

The Marketplace

By selling handmade crafts, our youth learn business management, finance, and marketing—skills that help them build a future in their own hometowns.
